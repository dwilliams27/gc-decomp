## Updated Conclusion

What was disproved:
- "There is no source pattern that produces two walkers from the same base."

What now looks true:
- The remaining problem is likely the exact addressing form used to derive the load/store walkers.
- Slot-pointer formulations tend to materialize an extra `addi ..., 0x1c`.
- Pure struct-pointer formulations tend to collapse back to the older 62-insn non-destructive family.

## Best Next Search Frontier

Focus on hybrids where:
- one side stays a struct pointer rooted at `gobj->user_data`
- the other side is derived in a way that encourages shared-base destructive walking
- but the compiler is not forced to materialize `&...gobjs[i]` as a separate `+0x1c` slot base

Concrete next directions:
- read side uses a slot-like expression, write side remains `store->gobjs[i]`
- delay slot derivation until after `i = 0; zero = i;`
- derive one walker from `store` and the other from an alias of `store`, but keep the access itself in `gobjs[i]` form rather than `*slot`

## Negative But Useful Small-Batch Findings

Small surgical experiments were run around the `54/62` winner:

- changing the post-`bl` order of `read_base = (char*) store`, `i = 0`, and `zero = i`
  - mostly preserved the same `54/62` shape
  - or regressed to `52/62`, `53/62`, or worse
- adding a temporary layer for `zero = i`
  - still preserved the same `54/62` plateau
- adding a second alias after `bl` (`copy`, `write_base`, etc.)
  - either preserved the same `54/62` codegen or regressed
- assigning `zero` inside the loop
  - dropped to `61` insns / `34/62`
- reloading `store` after the `bl`
  - collapsed badly (`64` insns, near-useless exact score)
- `register` keyword tests on the new `54/62` family
  - no effect at all across the tested subsets (`store`, `i`, `zero`, `read_base`, and pairs)

Practical takeaway:
- the `54/62` family is surprisingly stable
- the remaining lever is probably extremely local and non-obvious
